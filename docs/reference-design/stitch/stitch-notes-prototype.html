What belongs here

Not essays for the sake of essays. Just useful residue: context, memory, references, false starts, and the details that make a mix feel lived-in.

Notebook Entry 01 April 2026

Songs that need windshield time

Some tracks only reveal themselves in transit. Not on first listen, not at a desk, not while you’re skipping around to decide too fast. They need repetition, low light, and the kind of half-attention that lets details rise on their own.

Notebook Entry 02 Sequencing

The middle is where mixes usually fail

Openers are easy. Endings announce themselves. The hard part is the stretch where momentum can flatten into sameness. That’s usually where a mix needs one wrong-looking song that turns out to be exactly right.

Notebook Entry 03 Keepers

A note can be three lines and still earn its place

The point isn’t to perform taste in paragraph form. It’s to leave behind the small amount of context that survives re-reading: where the song landed, what it was paired with, and why it stayed.
